Living Cities reflects a belief that urban planning is not just about shaping space but about shaping futures. These projects explore how parametric tools can guide early decisions — decisions that ripple outward, influencing sustainability, livability and resilience. From analysing existing layouts to generating masterplans, each script becomes a lens to rethink how cities grow, adapt and respond.

The work spans multiple scales — from dense city blocks to individual floating homes — and applies to a variety of urban challenges: daylight access, heat island mitigation, typological diversity and facility distribution. Planning with purpose, from the ground up.

Shaping Light, Shaping Life — daylight availability and heat island analysis across city typologies · Typology matters: environmental metrics guiding habitability
Environmental Performance

Shaping Light, Shaping Life

These projects investigate how urban design influences comfort and environmental performance at street level. Daylight availability is analysed to assess its impact on heat island effects, outdoor comfort and spatial quality — from dense courtyards to individual floating homes.

Typology studies explore how the same urban context can yield radically different environmental outcomes. By analysing hundreds of block configurations, these projects reveal how design choices influence habitability — providing a variable for testing not just form, but holistic urban experience.

Typology exploration — 3D block models evaluated against environmental metrics and habitability targets · The viability game: balancing ambition and feasibility
Interactive Planning

Frameworks for Urban Decision-Making

A tool that transforms urban design into a field of experimentation. It produces masterplans based on parameters such as connectivity, typology, density, green distribution and access to facilities. Rather than prescribing solutions, it becomes an interactive urban planning platform — a way to test whether spatial targets can be met within a given plot and to uncover the trade-offs embedded in each decision.

A cluster of mixed-use developments requiring careful calibration of density, housing diversity and parking management. A generative approach producing hundreds of design iterations, each evaluated against performance metrics — balancing financial goals and site demands, between spatial logic and measurable outcomes.
