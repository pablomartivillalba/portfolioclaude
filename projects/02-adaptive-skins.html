Façade design has undergone a rapid transformation empowered by digital tools. Today's façades must manage sunlight, reduce overheating, cut costs and carbon, and still convey architectural identity. Parametric modelling helps reconcile these demands, embedding performance into the design from its earliest stages.

This set of three workflows addresses the full design lifecycle — from early-stage variable exploration to late-stage optimisation of specific façade systems and shading strategies. Each workflow is grounded in sustainability goals, ensuring that expressive choices are backed by technical rigour and environmental responsibility.

The outcome is not a fixed solution but a set of insights that support the designer in making informed choices while preserving creative freedom — sustainable creativity, backed up.

Workflow I · Informed Beginnings — key variable selection and performance criteria evaluation at early design stage
Workflow I

Informed Beginnings

This first workflow was conceived for early design stages, when many options are still available. By defining key variables, it generates alternative design options and evaluates them against selected performance criteria. Variables correspond directly to the building envelope — those defining façade orientation, module type and material choices.

The process simplifies decision-making by identifying key parameters and quantifying trade-offs, producing a catalogue of options with measurable sustainability outcomes — enabling early design choices with lasting environmental impact.

Workflow II · Left: perforated double-skin module variations · Right: stacked performance matrices — sun hours, views, wind exposure — driving Net Zero optimisation
Workflow II

Optimised Modular Envelopes

Developed for later design stages when the building geometry is largely fixed, this workflow focuses on the façade system under consideration and optimises its performance through a grading matrix. The process begins by evaluating the naked façade, synthesises results into a grading matrix, and adapts the solution to local conditions.

Beyond performance, the workflow integrates modularity and module selection, ensuring the design remains both efficient and buildable — driving the design process towards net zero without compromising architectural intent.

Workflow III · Shading Exploration — generative solar protection design with immediate performance feedback at façade and room scale
Workflow III

Shading Exploration

This tool investigates solar protection design through a generative process. Users can explore a catalogue of shading typologies and apply them directly to the project's façade. The script generates performance reports capturing metrics such as daylight availability, glare control and energy demand.

With immediate feedback, the design can be refined step by step — revealing the impact of each adjustment and turning shading strategies into a dynamic and responsive part of the design process.
