Spatial roof systems — space frames, gridshells and single-layer structures — sit at the intersection of structural rigour and architectural ambition. Their freeform geometries demand not only robust engineering analysis but also computational workflows capable of handling the complexity and scale that these structures entail at every stage of delivery.

At ONDDI, this work spans the full project lifecycle: from early feasibility assessment and rapid FEA using SAP2000, through generative form-finding with Kangaroo, to the coordination and documentation of systems composed of thousands of individual elements. Computational tools are not a convenience here — they are the only viable path to precision at scale.

The result is an approach where structural intuition and parametric logic reinforce each other, compressing iteration cycles and enabling confident decisions across design, fabrication and construction phases.

Form-finding — generative geometry exploration for freeform single-layer and gridshell structural systems
Structural Engineering

Feasibility at Speed

At tender stage, the ability to evaluate structural viability quickly and reliably is critical. Parametric models linked directly to SAP2000 allow multiple configurations — varying span, support conditions and structural depth — to be assessed against different regulatory standards in a compressed timeframe.

VBA-based automation routines streamline the repetitive aspects of this process, freeing engineering judgement for where it matters most: reading results, questioning assumptions and making decisions that balance performance with constructibility and cost.

Left: FEA model in SAP2000 — parametric geometry linked to structural analysis · Right: space frame node and member configuration
Generative Design

Form-Finding as Design Tool

For freeform roof geometries, form-finding strategies using Kangaroo allow structural logic to drive shape — producing surfaces where forces flow naturally through the material. These computationally derived geometries are not abstract explorations but working proposals grounded in structural behaviour, ready to be taken forward into analysis and detailing.

The parametric environment means that changes to boundary conditions, load patterns or material assumptions propagate instantly through the model — turning what would otherwise be weeks of manual iteration into a continuous, responsive design process.

Computational delivery — Grasshopper workflows coordinating thousands of structural elements, automating schedules, details and fabrication data
Computational Delivery

Precision at Scale

When a roof system is composed of thousands of unique elements, documentation is not a downstream task — it is a core engineering challenge. Grasshopper workflows extend beyond conceptual design to coordinate geometry, automate element schedules and generate fabrication data with the precision that complex spatial structures demand.

This computational layer connects structural intent directly to supply chain and construction: working in close alignment with project management, detailing, fabrication and on-site coordination teams, translating structural decisions into reliable, actionable information at every stage of delivery.
