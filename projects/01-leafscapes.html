Leafscapes was born from the challenge of merging performance and expression into a single architectural gesture. A series of leaf-shaped canopies were parametrised to provide solar protection and embrace the building in an organic movement that blurs the boundary between nature and structure.

The design demanded a careful balance: geometries had to mirror natural disorder while remaining typologically controlled, structurally feasible and buildable at large scale. Size and bi-directional curvature were defined as the primary generative variables, allowing an interactive tool to emerge — one that lets designers explore typologies, position canopies freely and test configurations in real time.

Each configuration was automatically translated into structural standards and loading analysis models, enabling architects and engineers to collaborate fluidly and receive instant feedback on structural feasibility — turning structure into a space for exploration.

Veinwork, seed and growth — deconstructing geometry as generative rules · Size and bi-directional curvature as the primary design variables
Design Rules

From Vein to Canopy

The project began by deconstructing geometry: studying the veinwork of a leaf, from seed to growth. These rules were translated into parametric logic — size and bi-directional curvature became the primary design variables governing both the formal identity and the structural character of each element.

Variations were generated by combining these two parameters across a typological range, producing a family of canopy forms that were formally diverse yet structurally consistent.

Organic layouts — compositions shading the promenades · Interactive tool for position, orientation and typology exploration
Structural Integration

Organic Layouts

With geometry under control, an interactive tool was developed to let designers play with different typologies, adjusting their position and orientation in space. Clusters of canopies could dominate the building while shaping shaded promenades that enhanced the natural setting.

What began as a structural necessity soon became a design opportunity. A refined version of the tool allowed designers to position and tilt columns freely, while a script translated each configuration into structural standards and loading analysis models — turning the structural process into a space for exploration.

Structural verification renders — wooden canopy structures in context · Rooted supports anchoring for unobstructed and functional layouts
