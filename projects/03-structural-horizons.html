Structural Horizons brings together a set of stadium roof projects that share the same underlying logic: vast, complex structures designed to shelter crowds while responding to bold architectural gestures. These geometries — often defined by sweeping spans, cantilevers and intricate load paths — demand tools capable of translating architectural intent into coherent structural forms.

Within interdisciplinary teams, my role was to develop parametric workflows that could automatically generate structural geometries in response to architects' primary design inputs. Once the structural logic is defined through key parameters, scripts ensure each variation of the roof geometry produces a consistent configuration — providing architectural freedom while guaranteeing that the structural form keeps pace with design iterations.

The workflow evolved beyond form generation: it integrated directly with structural analysis software, allowing design ideas to materialise as calculable models in real time — compressing weeks of back-and-forth into a continuous, integrated process.

Computational Approach

Geometry as Structure

Controlling geometry became the key to orchestrating a seamless conversation between architecture and engineering. Through parametric approaches, the expressive gestures of the design were translated instantly into structural logic — curves that speak and structure that listens.

The result was a live design environment where architects could make formal decisions while engineers immediately saw their structural implications. Each variation of the roof geometry produced a consistent structural configuration, providing architects the freedom to explore while guaranteeing buildability.

Engineering Precision

Curves Speak, Structure Listens

A refined parametric script allowed designers to position columns and adjust roof spans freely, while a secondary script automatically translated each configuration into structural standards and loading analysis models.

This seamless workflow enabled architects and engineers to collaborate in real time, testing ideas and receiving instant feedback on their feasibility — turning structure into a space for exploration rather than a constraint.
